My Houseplants

Haworthias

Succulents that can grow in medium light, unlike most that need lots of bright light.

Their translucent windows let the light in, allowing them to live in lower light than most other succulents. Also makes you want to take a bite.

This one had babies:

I'm still figuring out how to keep them so they will stop JETTISONING THEIR ROOTS OFF, but they're hardy plants and that will give me time to figure it out.

Purple Passion Plant

An adorable green plant with purple fuzzies! They like to stay moist, and will droop their leaves when they need more water. Very good if you're forgetful!

These plants started out about the same size, then I repotted one in a bigger pot, and it grew very fast. I took a few cuttings from the top to hopefully help it stop lengthening so quickly and focus on growing a little bushier

Pilea Glauca

Very cute tiny leaves, it's growing some long trailing limbs. My least problematic plant.

Hoya Krohniana "Black Leaves"

I've wanted a black houseplant for a while now, so I was very happy to get this little cutting. It's supposed to darken in higer light, but can tolerate lower light.

It's not pure black, but a mix of black and green. The teeny new leaf started out lime green, but has darkened a lot in the sun.

The Quarantine Zone

Where new plants go for a few weeks to make sure I'm not introducing pests to my collection.

I've been trying to stick to cuttings for new purchases, since they're both easier to check over for pests, and easier on my wallet.

Callisia Repens Pink Lady

Like Tradenscentia Zebrina, but pink, white, and green, and also tiny.

A bit of this fell off a plant at a nursery and they said I could keep it. It's separated into 3 teeny plants, and stuck in soil. Who will live and who will die is a mystery, but it's probably going to be the one on the bottom right if any.

Hoya Heuschkeiliana Variegated

Extremely pretty, new growth starts out pink and then fades into the normal white/green.

It got ROOT ROT when I tried to transfer it from sphagnum moss to coco husk. >:( Now I have to re-root it and hope this doesn't happen again.
